Pink Moon

In April's gentle nights, the pink moon graces the sky, Named for spring's first blooms, a beacon shining high. Not pink in hue, but rich in lore, from tribes of old it came, Marking earth's renewal, it carries spring's bright flame.

Though it glows a silver white, its heart is touched with spring, A promise of new life, a time for blossoming. Named for Phlox's pinkish blooms, it lights the season's way, A symbol of rebirth, a herald of the day.
