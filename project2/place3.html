Hundred Island

The Hundred Islands National Park in Alaminos, Pangasinan, is a breathtaking archipelago comprising 124 islands scattered across the azure waters of the Lingayen Gulf. This natural wonder is renowned for its stunning landscapes, featuring a mix of white sandy beaches, lush greenery, and unique rock formations. Popular islands like Governor's Island, with its panoramic viewing deck, Quezon Island's picnic spots, and the family-friendly Children's Island, offer diverse experiences for every type of traveler. Visitors can indulge in a range of activities, from island hopping and snorkeling to kayaking and diving, exploring the vibrant marine life beneath the clear waters. Equipped with facilities such as picnic areas, camping sites, and cottages, the park ensures a comfortable and enjoyable adventure. Easily accessible via a short boat ride from Lucap Wharf, the Hundred Islands National Park is a must-visit destination that showcases the natural splendor and rich biodiversity of the Philippines.
